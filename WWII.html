World War II

Dates: 1 September 1939 â€“ 2 September 1945

Causes: Treaty of Versailles, Expansionist Policies (Germany, Italy, Japan), Failure of Appeasement, Nationalism, Militarism

Major Battles: Battle of Stalingrad, Battle of Midway, D-Day (Normandy Invasion), Battle of the Bulge

Key Events: Invasion of Poland, Attack on Pearl Harbor, Battle of Britain, Holocaust, Atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki

Main Parties: Allies (including USA, UK, Soviet Union, China, France) vs. Axis Powers (Germany, Italy, Japan)

Main Leaders: Franklin D. Roosevelt (USA), Winston Churchill (UK), Joseph Stalin (Soviet Union), Adolf Hitler (Germany), Benito Mussolini (Italy), Emperor Hirohito (Japan)

Human Losses: Over 70 million people died, making it the deadliest conflict in human history.

Outcome: The war ended with the unconditional surrender of the Axis Powers, the creation of the United Nations, and the beginning of the Cold War between the USA and the Soviet Union.
