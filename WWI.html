World War I

Dates: 28 July 1914 â€“ 11 November 1918

Causes: Militarism, Alliances, Imperialism, Nationalism (MAIN)

Major Battles: Battle of the Somme, Battle of Verdun, Battle of Gallipoli

Key Events: Assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand, sinking of the Lusitania, Treaty of Versailles

Main Parties: Allies (including France, Russia, Britain, Italy, and later the United States) vs. Central Powers (Germany, Austria-Hungary, Ottoman Empire)

Main Leaders: Woodrow Wilson (USA), Georges Clemenceau (France), David Lloyd George (UK), Vittorio Orlando (Italy), Tsar Nicholas II (Russia), Kaiser Wilhelm II (Germany), Franz Joseph I (Austria-Hungary), Mehmed V (Ottoman Empire)

Human Losses: Over 9 million soldiers and 7 million civilians died as a result of the war.

Outcome: The war ended with the Treaty of Versailles, which imposed heavy penalties on Germany, leading to economic difficulties and ultimately contributing to the start of World War II.
